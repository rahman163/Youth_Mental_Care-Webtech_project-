Dr. Mayture Yap

Master of Clinical Psychology

Mayture is a practicing Clinical Psychologist whose focus is on providing psychological interventions that involves assessment, psychotherapy and trainings related to mental health issues. Her therapeutic services are delivered through an array of humanistic and cognitive behavioral techniques with the aim of facilitating individual's cognitive, emotional and psychosocial development.

Throughout her practice as a Clinical Psychologist, Mayture has various experiences on providing psychological assessments to evaluate personality, cognitive, behavioral and emotional functioning for both teenagers and adults. She also has various experiences working with women and children who are survivors of domestic violence as well as with the marginalized population, mainly refugees, by providing psychological intervention and trainings.

At present, Mayture is a private practitioner working closely with other mental health professionals such as Psychiatrists and Counsellors. She is also a co-founder of InPsych (Psychological & Counselling Services). Her areas of interests are on developing interpersonal & communication skills, building self-esteem, conflict resolution, obsessive-compulsive disorder and the management of anxiety and trauma related issues.

Dr. Keng Shian Ling

Ph.D. (Clinical Psychology), Duke University, USA

M.A. (Clinical Psychology), Duke University, USA

B.Sc. (Psychology and Biology), Duke University, USA

Dr. Keng Shian Ling is a clinical psychologist specialized in treating adult psychopathology, with a focus on mood disorders, anxiety disorders, emotion regulation issues, and borderline personality disorder She received her PhD in Clinical Psychology from Duke University (USA), and completed her clinical psychology residency at Centre for Addiction and Mental Health, Toronto, Canada. She holds an appointment as Associate Professor of Psychology at Yale-NUS College, and teaches courses in abnormal psychology and advanced clinical psychology. Additionally, she runs a research program focusing on examining the efficacy and mechanisms of change of mindfulness-based interventions, as well as the adaptation, implementation, and dissemination of empirically-supported interventions in Malaysia.

Clinically, Dr. Keng has received training in cognitive behavior therapy (CBT), dialectical behavior therapy (DBT), and other mindfulness-based approaches such as mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT). She has worked in hospital and clinic settings with patients of a variety of presentations, including mood, anxiety, substance use, eating, and personality disorders. She routinely provides training and workshops on DBT, and is fluent in English and Mandarin Chinese. Her clinical practice is culturally sensitive and LGBTQ friendly, and she aspires to help improve the quality and accessibility of mental health services in Southeast Asia through teaching, research, clinical work, and advocacy.
